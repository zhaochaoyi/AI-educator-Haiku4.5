How the Calculations Work

Savings Growth

Each year, we:

  1. Start with the previous year's savings balance
  2. Add your annual savings (12 × monthly contribution)
  3. Apply investment returns: New Balance = Balance × (1 + Return %)
  4. Deduct the major expense if it occurs that year

Income Growth

Your income increases each year: New Income = Income × (1 + Growth %)

Inflation

Your retirement spending is adjusted for inflation from the starting year using: Inflated Amount = Amount × (1 + Inflation %)^Years

Plan Status

On Track: Your projected savings at retirement exceed your estimated spending needs.

At Risk: Your savings may not be sufficient without additional planning.

Off Track: You would need to increase savings, delay retirement, or reduce spending.

Important Limitations

  • No taxes: This calculator doesn't account for income tax, capital gains tax, or investment taxes.
  • Fixed percentages: Real returns, growth, and inflation vary year to year.
  • No Social Security or pensions: Only your personal savings are modeled.
  • No withdrawals: After retirement, we assume you spend equally each year and don't model how long your savings will last.
  • Simple calculations: This is an educational tool, not a comprehensive financial plan.
